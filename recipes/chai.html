Chai

Description

Indian tea, also known as chai, is rich and milky, deeply coloured, steaming hot, and boldly flavourful with a definite need for sugar to offset the toasty bitterness. The word chai just means tea.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz water
  • 4 oz whole milk
  • granulated sugar to taste
  • 1 heaping tablespoon of black tea
  • 4 cardamom pods smashed with side of a knife
  • small piece of cinnamon stick
  • 1/4 " coin of fresh ginger
  • pinch of fennel seeds about 10 seeds

Steps

  1. Bring water and milk to a simmer with desired spices in a medium saucepan
  2. Reduce heat to lowest setting and add tea
  3. Steep until tea takes on a deep, pinky-tan colour, about 3 minutes, stirring occasionally
  4. Strain into a cup and stir in sugar to taste
